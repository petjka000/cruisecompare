About Africa & Canaries

Africa and the Canary Islands offer one of the world's most diverse cruising experiences, stretching from the dramatic coastlines of South Africa through the historic ports of North Africa to the volcanic islands of the Canary archipelago. This region delivers everything from world-class wildlife encounters—including opportunities to see the Big Five on safari-adjacent excursions—to rich Moorish architecture, pristine beaches, and world-renowned vineyards. The Canary Islands, a Spanish autonomous region just off Morocco's coast, provide an accessible European gateway into African culture, while South African cruises showcase the dramatic Cape of Good Hope and the vibrant waterfront culture of Cape Town. Whether you're seeking adventure, history, relaxation, or culinary experiences, this region rewards cruisers with extraordinary variety across relatively short sailing distances.

🚢 Top Ports in Africa & Canaries

Cape Town

Table Mountain, Robben Island, Constantia wine region

Often rated among the world's most beautiful cities, Cape Town offers dramatic landscapes with Table Mountain as its iconic backdrop. The V&A Waterfront provides extensive shopping and dining, while day trips to the Cape Winelands (Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, Constantia) deliver world-class wine experiences. Robben Island tours, where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned for 18 years, offer profound historical insights.

Casablanca

Hassan II Mosque, Morocco Mall, Rick's Café

Morocco's largest city combines French colonial architecture with traditional Moroccan design. The Hassan II Mosque—one of the largest mosques in the world with space for 105,000 worshippers—is a must-see, featuring intricate tilework and the world's tallest minaret. The nearby Morocco Mall offers luxury shopping, though the real treasure is exploring the narrow lanes of the old Medina for traditional crafts and spices.

Las Palmas (Gran Canaria)

Playa de las Canteras, Vegueta old town, Maspalomas Dunes

Gran Canaria's capital showcases the best of Canarian culture in its UNESCO-protected Vegueta district, where Christopher Columbus famously stopped on his voyages. The golden sands of Playa de las Canteras rank among Europe's best urban beaches, while the island's interior reveals dramatic mountain villages and the otherworldly Maspalomas sand dunes in the south.

Santa Cruz de Tenerife

Teide National Park, whale watching, Carnival celebrations

The largest of the Canary Islands, Tenerife is dominated by Mount Teide—a stratovolcano and Spain's highest peak at 3,718 metres. Cable car rides to near the summit offer breathtaking views, while the island's southern coasts provide excellent whale and dolphin watching opportunities. The capital hosts one of the world's largest Carnival celebrations each February.

Port Louis (Mauritius)

Seven Coloured Earth, Pamplemousses Botanical Garden, luxury resorts

This Indian Ocean paradise combines African, European, and Asian influences in a compact, accessible destination. The famous Seven Coloured Earth at Chamarel displays geologically unusual striped dunes in seven distinct hues, while the Pamplemousses Botanical Garden features giant water lilies and over 600 plant species. Mauritius excels at beach relaxation and high-end resort experiences.

🧳 What to Expect

☀️ Weather

Weather varies dramatically across the region. South African cruises (Nov-Feb) enjoy warm, dry summers with temperatures of 20-28°C in Cape Town and 25-32°C in Durban. The Canary Islands maintain temperate conditions year-round (18-24°C), ideal for those seeking mild sunshine. North African ports like Casablanca range from 15-22°C in winter. Pack layers for South Africa—summer afternoons can be surprisingly cool at the Cape—and sun protection across all destinations.

🍽️ Local Cuisine

Each sub-region offers distinct culinary traditions. South Africa is famous for braai (barbecue), biltong (dried cured meat), and Cape Malay curries in Cape Town's Bo-Kaap neighbourhood. Pair these with wines from Stellenbosch and Franschhoek, internationally recognised for their quality. Morocco showcases tagines, couscous, and fresh mint tea in ornate cafes. The Canaries feature mojo sauces, fresh fish (pescado fresco), and wrinkled potatoes (papas arrugadas). Don't miss a正宗 Moroccan tagine in Casablanca or fresh Cape Malay curry in Cape Town's Bo-Kaap—these regional specialties define the culinary identity of each port.

🏛️ Culture & Customs

Dress modestly when visiting mosques and religious sites in North Africa—cover shoulders and knees for women, avoid shorts for men. South Africa is more relaxed but safari excursions benefit from neutral-coloured clothing. Tipping is customary throughout the region: 10-15% in restaurants, €1-2 per bag for porters, and €5-10 for tour guides. In Morocco, bargaining in souks is expected and part of the shopping culture—start at 40-50% of the asking price. The Canaries follow Spanish customs with later dining times (lunch 2-3pm, dinner 9-11pm).

💰 Currency

Currency varies significantly: South Africa uses the Rand (ZAR), Morocco uses the Dirham (MAD), Mauritius uses the Mauritian Rupee (MUR), and the Seychelles uses the Rupee (SCR). The Euro is used in the Canary Islands and Ceuta/Melilla. Credit cards are widely accepted in tourist areas and on ships, but carry local currency for markets, tips, and small vendors. ATMs are available in all major ports—notify your bank before travelling to avoid card blocks.

🗣️ Language

English is the official language in South Africa and widely spoken in tourist areas, safari lodges, and on cruise excursions. In the Canary Islands, Spanish (Castellano) is primary, though most tourism workers speak basic English. Morocco uses Arabic and French—English is less common outside resorts, but major tourist sites have English-speaking guides. Mauritius is bilingual in English and French, with French often preferred. Learning a few local phrases is always appreciated.

💡 Insider Tips

  • ★For Cape Town, book the Table Mountain cable car tickets online in advance during peak season—queues can exceed 90 minutes, and weather closures are common. Alternatively, hike to the top for free if you're fit.
  • ★In Morocco, visit the medinas on the morning of a public holiday when most cruise ships are prohibited from docking—this gives you authentic access without the tourist crowds.
  • ★Fred Olsen's Braemar ship offers exceptional South Africa cruises with no single supplement on selected cabins—a significant saving for solo travellers interested in this region.
  • ★Purchase South African wine at the source in Stellenbosch rather than duty-free on the ship—expect to pay 40-60% less for the same vintage at the wine estates.
  • ★For whale watching in Tenerife, independent boat tours from Puerto Colón cost €35-50 compared to ship excursions at €75-90 for essentially the same experience.

🎒 Packing Tips

  • ✓Binoculars for wildlife viewing—essential for whale watching in the Canaries and birdlife in South African nature reserves
  • ✓Lightweight neutral-coloured clothing for South African safari-adjacent excursions and game reserves
  • ✓Modest clothing covering shoulders and knees for mosque visits in Morocco and North Africa
  • ✓Comfortable walking shoes with grip—the volcanic terrain of the Canaries and uneven cobblestones in old medinas demand sturdy footwear
  • ✓Light layers for Cape Town—afternoon winds from False Bay can drop temperatures dramatically even in summer
  • ✓High-SPF sunscreen (50+) and after-sun lotion—the African sun is significantly stronger than Northern European UV levels
  • ✓A light rain jacket or packable shell—Cape Town's Table Mountain creates its own weather systems, and afternoon showers are common in summer

  • ✓Swimwear for pool days and beach ports—the Indian Ocean islands and Canaries offer excellent swimming opportunities

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time of year to cruise Africa & Canaries?▼
November through February is ideal for South Africa, offering warm weather (20-28°C), minimal rain, and excellent wildlife viewing. The Canary Islands can be visited year-round, though May-June and September-October offer the best combination of good weather and fewer crowds. Avoid June-August for South African cruises as this is their winter with cooler temperatures (10-18°C) and increased rainfall in some regions. Christmas and New Year (mid-December to early January) commands premium pricing but offers festive atmosphere, especially in Cape Town.
Do I need a visa for Africa & Canaries cruises?▼
Visa requirements vary significantly by nationality and itinerary. EU, UK, US, Canadian, and Australian passport holders typically need no visa for: South Africa (tourist visa-free for up to 90 days), Canary Islands (as Spanish territory, covered by Schengen), Morocco (tourist visa-free for 90 days). However, Mauritius and Seychelles require visas on arrival for most Western passport holders (typically €25-50 fee). Check specific requirements for your nationality 6-8 weeks before departure, particularly for Madagascar and Mozambique if included in your itinerary. Your cruise line will provide documentation support but is not responsible for individual visa compliance.
How many days do I need in Africa & Canaries?▼
A minimum of 7 nights captures the highlights of either the Canaries/Morocco circuit or a focused South African coastal route. However, 10-14 nights allows for meaningful exploration of multiple sub-regions and fewer rushed port days. Consider adding 2-3 pre- or post-cruise land days in Cape Town to fully experience Table Mountain, the Winelands, and Robben Island. First-time Africa cruisers should prioritise Cape Town and one of the Canary Islands for the contrast of experiences. The Indian Ocean routes (Mauritius, Seychelles, Madagascar) typically require 10-14 nights minimum to make the long flight times worthwhile.
What should I budget for excursions in Africa & Canaries?▼
Excursion costs vary considerably: Budget tours (€30-60) include city walking tours, basic beach visits, and local market visits. Standard excursions (€70-120) cover half-day city highlights, temple/mosque visits with guides, and whale watching. Premium experiences (€150-300+) include Cape Winelands wine tastings, hot air balloon rides over the Maspalomas Dunes, full-day safari experiences, and submarine or semi-submersible reef tours. Free activities include beach time in the Canaries, exploring the V&A Waterfront in Cape Town, and walking the old medinas in Moroccan ports. Book ship excursions for guaranteed logistics or independent tours for 30-50% savings—ensure any independent operator provides proper insurance and has good reviews.
