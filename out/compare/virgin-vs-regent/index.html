Virgin Voyages vs Regent Seven Seas 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Virgin Voyages vs Regent Seven Seas 2026

Virgin Voyages and Regent Seven Seas represent two radically different philosophies in premium cruising. Virgin targets adults 18+ with contemporary design, vibrant nightlife, and inclusive pricing that covers dining and basic beverages. Regent Seven Seas operates in the ultra-luxury segment, offering all-suite accommodations, unlimited shore excursions, and comprehensive all-inclusive pricing including flights and transfers. Virgin's Scarlet Lady and sister ships bring a rebellious, festival-at-sea energy to the Caribbean and Mediterranean. Regent's smaller vessels prioritise refined elegance, destination immersion, and personalised service. Both deliver exceptional value within their respective tiers, yet appeal to entirely different traveller psychographics seeking either youthful energy or timeless sophistication.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
Value assessment depends entirely on your spending habits and priorities. Virgin Voyages offers strong inclusions at a lower entry price—dining, basic beverages, WiFi, and gratuities are covered, though shore excursions, premium drinks, and spa access cost extra. A typical Virgin voyage might total €2,000-2,500 per person when accounting for desired add-ons. Regent Seven Seas commands significantly higher upfront fares (€5,500+ for seven nights) yet includes virtually everything: flights, transfers, unlimited shore excursions, all beverages including premium wines and spirits, specialty dining, WiFi, and gratuities. For travellers who would book business-class flights, take multiple excursions, and enjoy wine with dinner, Regent frequently delivers superior net value despite the sticker shock.
Which for families?▼
Regent Seven Seas is the only viable option for families with children, as Virgin Voyages maintains a strict adults-only 18+ policy across all sailings. Regent welcomes guests of all ages and provides dedicated programming through Club Mariner for children aged 5-17 on select voyages, though families should note that Regent's sophisticated atmosphere and enrichment-focused itineraries appeal more to mature children and teenagers. The spacious all-suite accommodations work well for families requiring separate sleeping areas. Virgin's adults-only restriction is fundamental to its brand identity—parents seeking a child-free escape might consider Virgin for a couple's getaway while choosing Regent for true family holidays.
How do prices compare?▼
The pricing structures are fundamentally incomparable without examining total voyage cost. Virgin Voyages lists seven-night Caribbean or Mediterranean cruises from approximately €1,400 per person in an inside cabin, with Sea Terrace balcony cabins typically €1,800-2,200. RockStar Suites range €4,000-8,000. Regent Seven Seas begins at approximately €5,500 for seven nights in the smallest veranda suite, with Penthouse and higher categories reaching €8,000-15,000. However, Regent's fare includes elements Virgin charges separately: roundtrip flights (€800-2,000 value), airport transfers (€150), unlimited shore excursions (€150-400 daily value), all beverages (€80-150 daily), and prepaid gratuities (€200). Reverse-calculating equivalent inclusions often narrows the apparent gap considerably.
Which has better food?▼
Both lines excel gastronomically but with divergent philosophies. Virgin Voyages disrupted cruise dining by eliminating the main dining room entirely, offering 20+ distinct venues from Korean barbecue to experimental tasting menus, all included. The quality is genuinely restaurant-standard, with menus developed by Michelin-starred chefs and a casual, flexible approach—dine when and where you choose without reservations at most venues. Regent Seven Seas maintains classical cruise dining excellence through Compass Rose (main dining), specialty venues including Prime 7 steakhouse and Chartreuse French restaurant, and poolside casual options. Regent's culinary programme emphasises consistency, premium ingredients, and impeccable service rather than innovation. Virgin suits adventurous palates seeking variety; Regent rewards those who value flawless execution of familiar luxury.
Which for first-timers?▼
Virgin Voyages proves more accessible for cruise newcomers, particularly those aged 30-55 who might otherwise avoid cruising. The contemporary design feels familiar—more boutique hotel than traditional ship—while the adults-only environment eliminates concerns about children's programming or family-oriented activities. The flexible dining, casual dress code, and intuitive app-based experience reduce the learning curve associated with cruise conventions. First-timers seeking immediate comprehensive luxury without decision fatigue may prefer Regent Seven Seas, as the all-inclusive model removes spending anxiety entirely. Regent's smaller ships and higher crew ratios provide more personalised guidance for novices. Ultimately, Virgin attracts cruising-curious travellers from land-based holidays; Regent suits those wanting to commence with the industry's most refined product.
Main differences?▼
The fundamental distinction lies in target demographic and cruising philosophy. Virgin Voyages is a contemporary lifestyle brand applying cruise logistics to an adult-oriented resort experience—emphasising design, social connection, entertainment, and flexibility. The ships are large (2,770 guests), the atmosphere energetic, and the experience customisable through à la carte additions. Regent Seven Seas represents traditional ultra-luxury cruising refined to its apex—intimate ships (490-750 guests), anticipatory service, destination immersion through included excursions, and comprehensive pricing that eliminates transactional moments. Virgin asks guests to curate their experience; Regent delivers a complete, predetermined luxury product. One appeals to those seeking stimulation and choice; the other to those desiring serenity and seamlessness.
