Celebrity Cruises vs P&O Cruises 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Celebrity Cruises vs P&O Cruises 2026

Celebrity Cruises and P&O Cruises represent two distinct approaches to premium cruising from Europe. Celebrity, part of Royal Caribbean Group, delivers modern luxury with contemporary design, farm-to-table dining, and a cosmopolitan international atmosphere. P&O Cruises, Britain's favourite cruise line, offers traditional British cruising with familiar comforts, sterling currency onboard, and a distinctly home-from-home feel. Both operate mid-sized to large ships with strong Mediterranean and Caribbean programmes, yet appeal to different sensibilities. Celebrity targets affluent travellers seeking sophistication and culinary excellence, while P&O prioritises accessibility, British entertainment, and inclusive value for UK passengers wanting familiarity at sea.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
P&O Cruises typically offers superior headline value with lower base fares and included gratuities saving approximately €126 per couple weekly. However, Celebrity's pricing includes more sophisticated dining venues and premium touches as standard. When comparing like-for-like with drinks packages and specialty dining, the gap narrows considerably. P&O excels for budget-conscious travellers wanting predictable costs, while Celebrity rewards those who prioritise culinary and design investment. British passengers avoiding currency conversion may find P&O's sterling pricing advantageous. Ultimately, value depends on spending patterns: minimal spenders favour P&O, while those wanting premium experiences find Celebrity's inclusive luxury more economical than paying à la carte elsewhere.
Which for families?▼
P&O Cruises wins decisively for British families through The Reef programme, offering complimentary childcare from 9am to late evening with age-appropriate activities for 2-4, 5-8, 9-12, and 13-17 year groups. Night nursery for under-fives allows parents genuine evening freedom. Family cabins, dedicated children's tea times, and British school holiday alignment prove practical. Celebrity's Camp at Sea operates similarly but with shorter hours and stronger teen programming. Families prioritising sophisticated dining and contemporary spaces may prefer Celebrity, yet P&O's comprehensive, familiar approach reduces parental stress and maximises children's enjoyment through proven British programming.
How do prices compare?▼
P&O Cruises' seven-night Mediterranean inside cabins average €649 versus Celebrity's €899, representing a 28% premium for Celebrity. This differential widens with balcony cabins: approximately €1,199 versus €1,599. However, P&O's advantage diminishes when accounting for included gratuities on P&O (saving €126) versus Celebrity's additional charges. Drinks packages cost €45-55 daily on P&O versus €65 on Celebrity. Specialty dining averages €45-55 per person on Celebrity versus £25-35 on P&O. Ultimately, comparable experiences with drinks, gratuities, and two specialty meals narrow the gap to roughly 10-15%, with Celebrity commanding premium for design and culinary sophistication.
Which has better food?▼
Celebrity Cruises delivers superior culinary innovation through partnerships with Daniel Boulud and global chef collaborations, offering genuinely restaurant-quality experiences at Eden, Le Voyage, and Raw on 5. Main dining room menus feature more adventurous global cuisine with stronger vegetarian and plant-based options. P&O Cruises excels at consistent, crowd-pleasing British favourites—excellent roasts, curries, and comfort food executed reliably across the fleet. The Epicurean restaurant on Britannia and Arvia offers fine dining, though less ambitious than Celebrity's offerings. Food enthusiasts seeking discovery and culinary theatre prefer Celebrity; those wanting familiar, well-executed comfort food favour P&O's approachable consistency.
Which for first-timers?▼
P&O Cruises suits British first-timers through familiar currency, included gratuities eliminating tipping confusion, and culturally intuitive entertainment and dining. The atmosphere feels accessible rather than intimidating, with staff understanding British preferences and communication styles. Celebrity appeals to first-timers seeking immediate 'wow factor' through stunning ship design and elevated experiences that demonstrate cruising's potential. International passengers or those wanting contemporary luxury from the outset prefer Celebrity. Ultimately, nervous first-timers gain confidence through P&O's predictability, while adventurous newcomers wanting to understand premium cruising's possibilities find Celebrity more inspiring. Both offer excellent service; the choice depends on comfort with contemporary versus traditional environments.
Main differences?▼
The fundamental distinction lies in cultural orientation: Celebrity pursues international modern luxury with cosmopolitan sophistication, while P&O delivers British cruising tradition with accessible familiarity. Celebrity invests heavily in contemporary design, culinary innovation, and wellness programming; P&O prioritises entertainment value, children's facilities, and inclusive pricing. Celebrity operates in US dollars with additional gratuities; P&O uses sterling with tips included. Evening experiences diverge significantly: Celebrity's 'Evening Chic' replaces formal nights with smart casual elegance, while P&O maintains traditional Black Tie events. Passenger demographics differ accordingly—Celebrity attracts affluent international professionals, P&O draws British families and loyalists seeking consistent, value-driven holidays with home comforts.
