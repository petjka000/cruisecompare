MSC Cruises vs P&O Cruises 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > MSC Cruises vs P&O Cruises 2026

MSC Cruises and P&O Cruises represent two distinct approaches to contemporary cruising. MSC brings Italian-inspired international flair with a growing fleet of mega-ships featuring innovative design and family-focused amenities. P&O Cruises offers British heritage cruising with familiar comforts, refined service, and itineraries tailored to UK holidaymakers. Both operate in the mid-market segment but cater to different cultural preferences and travel expectations, making direct comparison essential for informed booking decisions in 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
P&O Cruises typically offers better headline value with gratuities included in the fare and GBP pricing eliminating exchange rate concerns. However, MSC frequently runs aggressive promotional campaigns with cabin upgrades, beverage packages, and onboard credit that can equalise total costs. Factor in that P&O's included drinks packages on select fares and traditional British inclusions like afternoon tea add value. MSC's à la carte pricing model requires careful calculation. For comparable seven-night Mediterranean cruises, expect P&O to run €50-100 cheaper base fare, but MSC's promotions often close this gap. Analyse total holiday cost including flights, transfers, and onboard spending rather than brochure prices alone.
Which for families?▼
MSC Cruises dominates for families through its industry-leading children's programming. The LEGO partnership creates dedicated play spaces, building competitions, and branded experiences across all ships. Baby Club for under-3s uses Chicco equipment; Mini Club (3-6), Juniors (7-11), and Teens (12-14, 15-17) offer age-appropriate activities until midnight. Family cabins accommodate up to six with connecting options. Waterparks, bowling, and F1 simulators engage all ages. P&O offers competent kids' clubs but smaller facilities and less imaginative programming. MSC's multilingual environment suits internationally-minded children, while P&O's British focus may comfort those wanting familiar surroundings. For pure family facilities and value, MSC wins decisively.
How do prices compare?▼
P&O Cruises generally price 10-15% lower than MSC for equivalent cabin categories on comparable itineraries. A seven-night Mediterranean inside cabin averages €699 with P&O versus €799 with MSC in 2026. However, pricing structures differ significantly: P&O includes service charges in fares, while MSC adds €12-14 daily. P&O operates in GBP, protecting UK customers from Euro fluctuations. MSC's dynamic pricing and frequent promotions—including 'All-In' packages with drinks and Wi-Fi—can reverse the equation. Suite categories show narrower gaps. Caribbean fly-cruises from P&O and MSC's Miami departures require careful total-cost comparison including flights. Book early with P&O for best fares; watch MSC's promotional windows for deals.
Which has better food?▼
P&O Cruises wins for consistent quality and British palates, with main dining rooms serving reliable comfort food, excellent curry nights, and afternoon tea standards. Celebrity chef partnerships with Marco Pierre White (Ocean Grill) and Eric Lanlard (patisserie) elevate specialty dining. MSC offers more adventurous Mediterranean and international cuisine that can polarise—superb when executed, occasionally inconsistent. Their buffet quantity impresses but quality varies by ship age. Both lines struggle with mass-catering limitations; neither matches premium lines. Foodies should budget for specialty restaurants on either. P&O's included dining satisfies traditionalists; MSC's European approach rewards adventurous eaters willing to navigate occasional inconsistency.
Which for first-timers?▼
P&O Cruises suits first-timers wanting familiar, low-risk introduction with British currency, included tips, understandable dress codes, and Southampton departures eliminating flight complications. The structured, traditional experience provides clear expectations. MSC appeals to first-timers seeking contemporary resort-style cruising with impressive ship facilities, flexible dining, and international atmosphere, but requires more navigation of à la carte pricing and multilingual environment. P&O's crew training emphasises hospitality for cruising novices; MSC's service can feel more transactional. First-timers prioritising ease and predictability should choose P&O; those wanting impressive modern ships and don't mind learning cruise conventions should consider MSC. Both offer accessible entry points to cruising.
Main differences?▼
Cultural identity defines the fundamental difference: MSC delivers Italian-led European international cruising with multilingual announcements, Euro pricing, and Mediterranean-influenced service rhythms; P&O provides distinctly British experience with GBP, UK plug sockets, Strictly Come Dancing screenings, and roast dinner Sundays. Operationally, MSC builds larger, newer ships with cutting-edge entertainment technology and family infrastructure; P&O maintains more moderate sizing with traditional cruise architecture and refined public spaces. Pricing philosophy diverges—P&O's more inclusive fares versus MSC's à la carte model. Evening atmosphere contrasts sharply: MSC's flexible resort casual versus P&O's structured formal nights. Itinerary deployment differs too—MSC's global network versus P&O's UK-centric focus with Caribbean fly-cruises. These distinctions outweigh superficial similarities as both mid-market operators.
