Princess Cruises vs Virgin Voyages 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Princess Cruises vs Virgin Voyages 2026

Princess Cruises, a venerable name in ocean cruising since 1965, offers traditional cruise experiences with broad appeal across generations. Virgin Voyages, launched in 2021 by Richard Branson, disrupts the industry with adults-only voyages, contemporary design, and inclusive pricing. This comparison examines how these distinctly different cruise lines stack up for 2026 sailings, helping travellers identify which brand aligns with their vacation priorities, budget constraints, and travel companions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
Virgin Voyages typically delivers superior value when total vacation cost is calculated. While Princess advertises competitive base fares starting around €799 for seven nights, Virgin's €699 average includes gratuities (€112 value), basic Wi-Fi (€105 value), and all specialty dining. Princess passengers frequently spend €300-400 additional on these essentials. However, Princess offers promotional packages that narrow this gap. Value assessment ultimately depends on your consumption patterns: minimal spenders may find Princess cheaper, while those wanting comprehensive inclusions benefit from Virgin's transparent pricing structure without nickel-and-diming.
Which for families?▼
Princess Cruises is exclusively suitable for families, as Virgin Voyages maintains a strict adults-only policy prohibiting all guests under 18. Princess provides dedicated youth programmes across three age groups (3-7, 8-12, 13-17), family-friendly shore excursions, connecting staterooms, and partnerships with Discovery and Animal Planet for educational entertainment. Family pools, movie nights, and youth counsellors ensure children remain engaged throughout voyages. Virgin explicitly markets to adult travellers, with no facilities, programming, or accommodations for minors. Families considering Virgin must seek alternative cruise lines entirely.
How do prices compare?▼
Published base fares appear comparable: Princess averages €799 and Virgin €699 for seven-night inside cabins in 2026. However, pricing structures diverge significantly. Princess operates à la carte pricing with additional charges for gratuities (€16/person/day), Wi-Fi packages (€15/day), specialty dining (€25-60), and beverages. Virgin bundles these as standard. A Princess cabin ultimately costs €1,100-1,300 total versus Virgin's €699-900 for equivalent inclusions. Princess occasionally offers 'Princess Plus' and 'Princess Premier' packages that include drinks, Wi-Fi, and gratuities for €40-60 daily supplements, achieving parity with Virgin's model.
Which has better food?▼
Virgin Voyages dominates culinary offerings through its 'no main dining room, no buffet, no extra charge' philosophy. Twenty-plus restaurants include concepts by Michelin-starred chefs: Korean barbecue at Gunbae, experimental tasting at Test Kitchen, and elevated Italian at Extra Virgin. All venues are complimentary. Princess maintains traditional cruise dining with main dining rooms, buffet, and specialty restaurants incurring surcharges (€25-60). While Princess food quality is respectable, Virgin's innovative menus, diverse cuisines, and elimination of upcharges create superior gastronomic experiences. Food enthusiasts consistently rate Virgin higher for creativity, presentation, and value, though Princess satisfies traditionalists preferring familiar cruise dining conventions.
Which for first-timers?▼
First-time cruisers' ideal choice depends on their travel profile. Princess offers gentle introduction through familiar structures: assigned dining times, organised activities, and predictable rhythms that reduce decision fatigue. Extensive staff guidance and traditional hospitality reassure nervous newcomers. Virgin suits adventurous first-timers comfortable with ambiguity, preferring app-based planning, flexible schedules, and contemporary environments. Virgin's inclusive pricing simplifies budgeting by eliminating surprise costs that often frustrate cruise novices. Princess requires more vigilance tracking extras. Ultimately, conventional travellers favour Princess; experience-seekers embracing modern hospitality trends thrive with Virgin's unconventional approach.
Main differences?▼
Fundamental distinctions span demographics, philosophy, and structure. Princess welcomes all ages; Virgin restricts to adults 18+. Princess follows traditional cruise conventions: fixed dining, formal nights, gratuity envelopes, and structured entertainment. Virgin eliminates these entirely for flexible, casual experiences. Princess charges separately for Wi-Fi, dining venues, and crew tips; Virgin bundles these. Princess emphasises maritime heritage and global itineraries including Alaska and world voyages; Virgin focuses shorter Caribbean/Mediterranean routes with festival-style programming. Princess cabins prioritise function; Virgin staterooms feature innovative designs including convertible seabeds. Service styles differ: Princess offers attentive traditional hospitality; Virgin provides relaxed, peer-to-peer interactions. These philosophical divergences create entirely different vacation experiences despite both operating mid-market cruise products.
