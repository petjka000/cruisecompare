Norwegian Cruise Line vs P&O Cruises 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Norwegian Cruise Line vs P&O Cruises 2026

Norwegian Cruise Line and P&O Cruises represent two distinct approaches to mainstream cruising. Norwegian pioneered Freestyle Cruising, eliminating fixed dining times and dress codes for maximum flexibility. P&O Cruises, Britain's most established cruise line, delivers traditional maritime heritage with a distinctly British flavour. Both operate mid-market contemporary ships, yet cater to fundamentally different passenger expectations. Norwegian targets international travellers seeking casual, resort-style holidays with extensive entertainment and specialty dining. P&O focuses on British cruisers wanting familiar comforts, formal evenings, and understated elegance. Your choice depends on whether you prioritise spontaneous freedom or structured tradition, American-style spectacle or British refinement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
P&O Cruises typically offers better base value with lower starting fares around €699 for seven nights, though Norwegian's Free at Sea promotions can equalise costs when drinks packages, specialty dining credits, and Wi-Fi are included. Norwegian's €799 average rises significantly with gratuities at €16-20 daily and expensive specialty restaurants. P&O's more inclusive main dining experience and lower service charges at £7-8 daily reduce onboard spending pressure. British passengers particularly benefit from P&O's home currency pricing and absence of US-style tipping culture. However, Norwegian delivers superior entertainment and facilities for the fare. Value depends on your spending discipline and which inclusions you actually use.
Which for families?▼
Norwegian Cruise Line decisively wins for families with children of all ages. Their Splash Academy accommodates infants through teenagers with age-appropriate programming from 6 months to 17 years, while Entourage teen clubs feature dedicated hangout spaces and supervised activities. Waterparks, ropes courses, and go-karts provide physical outlets impossible on P&O vessels. Freestyle dining eliminates stress when children cannot sit through traditional two-hour meals. P&O's The Reef kids' club operates effectively but with more limited hours and facilities, particularly for teenagers who often find insufficient dedicated space. Norwegian's interconnecting family cabins and suite complexes specifically designed for multigenerational travel further cement their family advantage.
How do prices compare?▼
P&O Cruises maintains approximately €100 lower base pricing for comparable seven-night inside cabins, with Norwegian averaging €799 versus P&O's €699. However, direct comparison proves complicated by different pricing models. Norwegian aggressively promotes Free at Sea offers bundling drinks packages, specialty dining, Wi-Fi, and excursion credits that would cost €400-600 if purchased separately. P&O sells more a la carte, with lower entry points but charges for similar amenities. Gratuities differ substantially: Norwegian's €16-20 daily exceeds P&O's £7-8. Norwegian specialty restaurants typically cost €25-50 per person; P&O's alternative dining rarely exceeds €35. British passengers should also consider currency fluctuation exposure with Norwegian's dollar-based pricing.
Which has better food?▼
Both lines offer competent mainstream dining with distinct philosophical differences. Norwegian provides greater variety through Freestyle Cruising's multiple complimentary venues and extensive specialty restaurant portfolio spanning Cagney's Steakhouse to Teppanyaki, though quality varies considerably and premium venues incur significant surcharges. Their main dining rooms suffer from inconsistency due to high passenger volumes. P&O concentrates investment in fewer venues, delivering more reliable quality in main dining rooms with British-influenced menus, proper afternoon tea service, and notably superior bread and pastry execution. Foodies prioritising culinary adventure prefer Norwegian's options and flexibility; traditionalists valuing consistency and classical technique favour P&O's more disciplined approach. Neither competes with luxury lines for gastronomic excellence.
Which for first-timers?▼
P&O Cruises generally suits first-time cruisers better, particularly British passengers unfamiliar with cruise conventions. Their structured environment—with set dining times, formal nights, and clear expectations—provides reassuring framework for understanding cruise etiquette. British crew and predominantly British passengers eliminate cultural navigation challenges, while home currency pricing feels transparent. Norwegian's freestyle approach, while liberating for experienced cruisers, can overwhelm newcomers with excessive choice and hidden costs. However, younger first-timers or those intimidated by formal dress requirements may prefer Norwegian's casual resort atmosphere. P&O's smaller ships also prove less daunting for navigating initially. Consider your comfort with structure versus spontaneity when deciding.
Main differences?▼
The fundamental distinction lies in operational philosophy: Norwegian invented Freestyle Cruising eliminating fixed schedules, dress codes, and traditional cruise structure entirely, while P&O preserves classic maritime traditions including formal evenings and assigned dining. Norwegian operates larger, more spectacular ships featuring Broadway entertainment, thrill attractions, and extensive specialty dining requiring surcharges. P&O emphasises understated British elegance, sophisticated entertainment, and inclusive main dining without constant upselling. Demographically, Norwegian attracts younger international families and active travellers; P&O draws older British couples seeking familiar comfort. Norwegian's pricing appears higher initially but bundles promotions; P&O's lower base fares accumulate costs more transparently. These differences reflect American resort culture versus British club culture transplanted to sea.
