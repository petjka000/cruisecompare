P&O Cruises vs Regent Seven Seas 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > P&O Cruises vs Regent Seven Seas 2026

P&O Cruises and Regent Seven Seas represent fundamentally different approaches to ocean cruising. P&O Cruises, Britain's favourite cruise line, offers accessible, family-friendly voyages with a distinctly British atmosphere and competitive pricing. Regent Seven Seas operates in the ultra-luxury segment, delivering all-inclusive, all-suite experiences with impeccable service and exotic itineraries. This comparison examines how these contrasting lines stack up for 2026 sailings, helping you determine whether P&O's value-driven British charm or Regent's uncompromising luxury better suits your travel aspirations and budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
Value depends entirely on your priorities. P&O Cruises offers exceptional value for families and budget-conscious travellers, with seven-night Mediterranean cruises from €799 including meals and entertainment. However, Regent Seven Seas delivers superior value for luxury seekers when you account for inclusions: flights, unlimited shore excursions, premium drinks, gratuities, and WiFi would add €2,000-3,000 to a P&O fare. For 2026, Regent's 'Ultimate All-Inclusive' genuinely eliminates onboard spending, whilst P&O passengers typically spend €400-600 extra on drinks, excursions, and speciality dining. Calculate your total holiday cost rather than comparing base fares alone.
Which for families?▼
P&O Cruises dominates for families with children of any age. Their ships feature Ofsted-registered nurseries for 6 months+, Splashers (2-4), Surfers (5-8), Scubas (9-12), and H2O (13-17) clubs, all complimentary until midnight. Family cabins accommodate five, and flexible dining suits irregular schedules. Regent Seven Seas actively discourages children under 12, with no dedicated facilities, early dining times, and an adult-oriented atmosphere. Their smallest suites start at 28sqm versus P&O's family cabins at 18-25sqm with pullman beds. For multi-generational groups, P&O's Iona and Arvia offer the most comprehensive family infrastructure in cruising.
How do prices compare?▼
The price differential is substantial and structural. P&O's 2026 seven-night Mediterranean inside cabins average €799-1,099, balconies €1,299-1,799, and suites €2,499-3,999. Regent Seven Seas' entry-level Veranda Suites start at €6,500-8,500 for comparable itineraries, rising to €15,000+ for Penthouse and above. However, Regent includes flights (€400-800), unlimited shore excursions (€800-1,500 value), all drinks (€600-1,000), gratuities (€150), and WiFi (€150). A P&O cruise with comparable additions costs €3,500-4,500, narrowing the genuine gap to €2,000-4,000 for a fundamentally different experience on ships one-tenth the size with superior service.
Which has better food?▼
Regent Seven Seas offers superior culinary quality with ingredients sourced from La Réserve de Beaulieu and Le Cordon Bleu partnerships, served in intimate venues without surcharges. Their speciality restaurants Chartreuse (French), Prime 7 (steakhouse), and Sette Mari (Italian) match land-based fine dining. P&O Cruises delivers excellent mass-market dining with celebrity chef partnerships (Marco Pierre White, Eric Lanlard) and speciality restaurants like The Epicurean and Sindhu, but these incur €25-45 cover charges. Main dining room quality is comparable, though Regent's open-seating, no-reservation policy and unlimited lobster, caviar, and vintage wines create a more indulgent experience for gastronomes.
Which for first-timers?▼
P&O Cruises provides the ideal introduction to cruising for British first-timers. The familiar environment—sterling pricing, UK plug sockets, British TV channels, and staff who understand British humour—eliminates cultural adjustment. Lower financial commitment (€799 versus €6,500) reduces risk if cruising proves unsuitable. Extensive facilities help newcomers discover preferences: formal nights, theatre shows, or quiet lounges. Regent Seven Seas, whilst flawless, sets impossibly high expectations; subsequent cruises feel disappointing. However, if budget permits, Regent's inclusive model eliminates the confusion of onboard spending decisions that overwhelm some first-timers. For nervous cruisers, Regent's small ships and attentive service remove all friction.
Main differences?▼
Scale fundamentally distinguishes these lines: P&O operates ships carrying 1,800-5,200 guests with 1,200-1,800 crew; Regent's largest vessel carries 750 guests with 550 crew. This creates divergent atmospheres—bustling and sociable versus intimate and tranquil. Pricing philosophy differs absolutely: P&O employs à la carte pricing with abundant opportunities to spend more; Regent's all-inclusive model prohibits additional charges except spa, casino, and premium excursions. Demographics diverge sharply: P&O attracts families and retirees across income levels; Regent's minimum age policy and pricing select affluent, well-travelled couples. Itinerarily, P&O focuses on accessible Mediterranean and Caribbean routes; Regent emphasises remote destinations with extended port stays and overnight visits unavailable to larger vessels.
