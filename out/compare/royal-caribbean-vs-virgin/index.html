Royal Caribbean vs Virgin Voyages 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Royal Caribbean vs Virgin Voyages 2026

Royal Caribbean and Virgin Voyages represent two distinct philosophies in contemporary cruising. Royal Caribbean, a 55-year industry titan, operates 28 ships including the world's largest vessels, emphasizing scale, variety, and multigenerational appeal with water parks, Broadway shows, and extensive children's programming. Virgin Voyages, launched in 2021 by Richard Branson, reimagines cruise culture exclusively for adults 18+, prioritizing boutique hotel aesthetics, wellness-focused dining, and nightlife over traditional cruise conventions. Both occupy similar price brackets but serve fundamentally different traveller psychologies: Royal Caribbean rewards those seeking maximum entertainment density, while Virgin attracts experience-seekers valuing design-forward spaces and social atmosphere without family infrastructure.

Verdict by Traveller Type

FamiliesRoyal Caribbean

Adventure Ocean kids' program (6 months-17 years), teen-only spaces, family staterooms, and water parks make Royal Caribbean unmatched for multigenerational travel. Virgin's adult-only policy automatically disqualifies it.

Couples 25-40Virgin Voyages

Design-forward cabins, included dining at 20+ restaurants, no screaming children, and vibrant nightlife align with this demographic's preferences. The inclusive pricing eliminates nickel-and-diming friction.

Couples 50+Royal Caribbean

While Virgin attracts this age group, Royal Caribbean's traditional service structure, accessible entertainment, and loyalty program familiarity often resonate more. Suite accommodations and priority boarding reduce scale disadvantages.

Solo travellersVirgin Voyages

Single cabins without solo supplement penalties, social programming designed for connection, and inclusive pricing create lower barriers. The adult environment facilitates easier conversation and community formation.

Adventure seekersRoyal Caribbean

Royal Caribbean's private destinations (Perfect Day at CocoCay, Labadee), shore excursion breadth, and shipboard activities (rock climbing, FlowRider, skydiving simulator) exceed Virgin's more limited adventure portfolio.

Food enthusiastsVirgin Voyages

No buffet, no main dining room, 20+ included restaurants with menus by Michelin-starred chefs, and experimental concepts like Korean BBQ and test kitchens. Royal Caribbean's included dining remains cafeteria-influenced despite improvements.

Budget-consciousVirgin Voyages

While base fares appear similar, Virgin's inclusion of gratuities (€126-140/week saved), WiFi (€105-140/week saved), and all dining eliminates hidden costs that inflate Royal Caribbean's final price significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
Virgin Voyages typically delivers superior value when accounting for inclusions. A €699 Virgin fare includes gratuities (approximately €126-140 per person weekly), basic WiFi (€105-140), and all dining including specialty restaurants. Royal Caribbean's €799 base fare excludes these, adding €230-280+ in unavoidable costs. However, Royal Caribbean's unlimited drink packages (€65-75/day) can outperform Virgin's à la carte pricing for heavy drinkers. Families find Royal Caribbean value through included kids' programming that would require external childcare costs. Ultimately, Virgin rewards travellers who consume moderately on beverages; Royal Caribbean rewards those maximizing included entertainment and traveling with children who use Adventure Ocean extensively.
Which for families?▼
Royal Caribbean exclusively. Virgin Voyages' adult-only 18+ policy prohibits children entirely. Royal Caribbean's Adventure Ocean divides children into age-appropriate groups (6-36 months Aquatots, 3-5 Explorers, 6-8 Voyagers, 9-11 Navigators, 12-14 Teens, 15-17 Older Teens) with dedicated facilities and staff. Teen spaces include nightclubs, lounges, and activities. Family accommodations range from interior cabins to multi-room suites. Onboard, Royal Caribbean provides water parks, carousel, ice skating, surf simulators, and character meet-and-greets. Dining includes children's menus, early seating, and flexible My Time Dining. Virgin Voyages simply cannot accommodate families, making this comparison moot for parents.
How do prices compare?▼
Published fares appear comparable—Royal Caribbean 7-night Caribbean/Mediterranean interiors average €799, Virgin €699. However, total cost diverges significantly. Virgin's fare includes gratuities, WiFi, all dining, and fitness classes. Royal Caribbean adds €16-18 daily gratuities (€112-126 weekly), WiFi at €15-20 daily (€105-140), and specialty dining at €35-65 per person. A couple on Royal Caribbean faces €434-532 in additional fixed costs versus Virgin's inclusive pricing. Beverage costs vary: Royal Caribbean's drink package (€65-75 daily) suits heavy drinkers; Virgin's à la carte cocktails (€8-14) benefit moderate consumers. Shore excursions and spa treatments remain extra on both. Virgin's no-single-supplement cabins also advantage solo travellers.
Which has better food?▼
Virgin Voyages decisively. Royal Caribbean operates traditional cruise dining: main dining room with rotating menus, Windjammer buffet, and specialty restaurants charging premiums. Food quality ranges from acceptable to good, with specialty venues (Jamie's Italian, Chops Grille) outperforming included options. Virgin eliminated buffets and main dining rooms entirely, offering 20+ restaurants all included: Korean BBQ, experimental test kitchens, elevated Mexican, steakhouse, and more. Menus involve Michelin-starred chefs and James Beard award winners. Virgin's food philosophy emphasizes quality ingredients, contemporary preparation, and restaurant-specific concepts rather than cruise-standardized offerings. The included model encourages exploration without financial penalty. Royal Caribbean's food satisfies; Virgin's surprises and delights.
Which for first-timers?▼
Depends on traveller profile. First-timers seeking familiar cruise conventions—assigned dining times, Broadway-style entertainment, structured activities, and clear ship navigation—find Royal Caribbean more accessible. Its scale delivers comprehensive cruise education: formal nights, Captain's welcome, traditional debarkation procedures. Families with cruise-curious children particularly benefit. Conversely, first-timers intimidated by cruise stereotypes (buffet crowds, enforced socialization, rigid schedules) discover Virgin Voyages reimagines the experience entirely: no announcements, no assigned seating, no dress codes, inclusive pricing. Virgin suits confident travellers comfortable with ambiguity and self-directed exploration. Royal Caribbean provides training-wheels cruising; Virgin assumes cruise literacy or adventurous disposition. Neither punishes beginners, but expectations must align.
Main differences?▼
Philosophy and execution diverge fundamentally. Royal Caribbean maximizes: largest ships, most passengers, extensive entertainment, global itineraries, multigenerational appeal. It operates within traditional cruise parameters refined over decades. Virgin Voyages edits: adults-only, moderate capacity, inclusive pricing, contemporary design, flexible structures. Royal Caribbean builds floating cities; Virgin builds floating boutique hotels. Operationally, Royal Caribbean charges à la carte for experiences Virgin includes. Culturally, Royal Caribbean accommodates families and traditionalists; Virgin targets experience-seeking adults comfortable with nightlife, social atmosphere, and unconventional cruise formats. Royal Caribbean's loyalty program and repeat passenger infrastructure reward commitment; Virgin's relatively recent launch limits legacy benefits but also eliminates accumulated expectations. Both execute well within their distinct frameworks.
