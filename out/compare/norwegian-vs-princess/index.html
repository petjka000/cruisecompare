Norwegian Cruise Line vs Princess Cruises 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Norwegian Cruise Line vs Princess Cruises 2026

Norwegian Cruise Line and Princess Cruises represent two distinct approaches to contemporary cruising, both occupying the premium-mid market segment. Norwegian pioneered Freestyle Cruising, eliminating fixed dining times and dress codes for maximum flexibility. Princess, now under Carnival Corporation ownership, emphasises refined elegance with its OceanMedallion technology and destination-focused itineraries. Both lines operate globally with modern fleets, yet appeal to different traveller psychologies. Norwegian attracts younger, independent guests seeking variety and spontaneity. Princess cultivates loyal repeat passengers valuing traditional service structures and enrichment programming. Your ideal match depends on whether you prioritise unstructured freedom or curated sophistication.

Verdict by Traveller Type

FamiliesNorwegian Cruise Line

Norwegian's Splash Academy accommodates ages 6 months-12 years with complimentary programming, plus Entourage teen lounge. Multiple included dining options satisfy picky eaters, and adrenaline attractions like waterslides and race tracks engage multi-generational groups.

CouplesPrincess Cruises

Princess offers superior sanctuary spaces like The Sanctuary adults-only retreat, romantic balcony dining, and enrichment programmes fostering connection. The intimate, refined atmosphere suits couples prioritising quality time over high-energy entertainment.

Solo TravellersNorwegian Cruise Line

Norwegian leads the industry with 1,000+ solo cabins across multiple ships, dedicated solo lounges, and singles meetups. Freestyle dining eliminates awkward table-sharing, while vibrant nightlife facilitates spontaneous social connections.

Mature Travellers (65+)Princess Cruises

Princess delivers accessible enrichment, slower-paced itineraries with more sea days, and traditional service structures. The OceanMedallion simplifies navigation for less tech-confident guests, while the demographic skews older with appropriate entertainment.

Adventure SeekersNorwegian Cruise Line

Norwegian's ships feature go-kart tracks, laser tag, ropes courses, and virtual reality complexes. The Waterfront promenade brings ocean proximity to dining, while expedition-style excursions complement the active onboard environment.

Cultural EnthusiastsPrincess Cruises

Princess invests heavily in destination immersion through Discovery at SEA partnerships, regional cuisine development, and onboard naturalists. The enrichment programme exceeds industry standards for educational depth and authenticity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
Princess Cruises generally offers superior base value with lower entry pricing (€699 vs €799 for 7-night inside cabins) and comparable inclusions. However, Norwegian's Freestyle Cruising eliminates hidden costs—no specialty restaurant required for variety, no formalwear purchases, and flexible dining without gratuity-pressure. Princess nickel-and-dimes more aggressively: specialty restaurants, enhanced desserts, and certain activities carry surcharges. Norwegian's beverage packages and dining bundles often price competitively when pre-purchased. For budget-conscious travellers who resist upselling, Princess's traditional structure feels more transparent. For those wanting comprehensive choice without incremental spending, Norwegian's model ultimately delivers better experiential value despite higher upfront costs.
Which for families?▼
Norwegian Cruise Line decisively wins for families, particularly those with children under 12 or teenagers. The Splash Academy operates extensive hours with age-appropriate programming at no charge, while Entourage provides teens dedicated unsupervised spaces—rare industry offerings. Norwegian's diverse included dining accommodates selective eaters without specialty restaurant dependency. Adrenaline attractions (waterslides, race tracks, ropes courses) engage multiple generations simultaneously. Princess's Camp Discovery improved significantly post-2017 refurbishment but remains secondary, with limited teen facilities and earlier programme closure. Princess suits families prioritising educational travel and quieter evenings; Norwegian dominates for energy, variety, and genuine multi-generational appeal where grandparents, parents, and children all find satisfaction.
How do prices compare?▼
Princess Cruises consistently undercuts Norwegian by 10-15% on base fares—€699 versus €799 for comparable 7-night Mediterranean inside cabins in 2026. This gap widens in balcony categories where Norwegian's premium for The Haven suites and waterfront locations becomes pronounced. However, total vacation cost reverses this equation. Norwegian bundles more value: multiple included dining venues, superior complimentary entertainment, and fewer mandatory surcharges. Princess recoups through specialty dining pressure, premium desserts, and activity fees. Gratuities run €2-4 lower daily on Princess. Beverage packages favour Norwegian when purchased pre-cruise. Shore excursion pricing aligns closely. Ultimately, Princess wins on sticker price; Norwegian on comprehensive value.
Which has better food?▼
Princess Cruises delivers superior culinary consistency, particularly in main dining rooms where execution reliability exceeds Norwegian's variable standards. Princess's partnership with Chef Curtis Stone elevates specialty dining, while Alfredo's Pizzeria and International Café provide genuinely excellent complimentary options. Norwegian triumphs in variety and innovation: the Freestyle model supports 10+ included venues on larger ships, from teppanyaki to Brazilian churrascaria concepts without surcharges. Quality fluctuates more dramatically—outstanding at Cagney's Steakhouse, mediocre in main dining rooms. Foodies seeking authentic excellence should choose Princess; adventurous eaters wanting constant novelty prefer Norwegian. Both lag behind Celebrity and Holland America in absolute culinary achievement.
Which for first-timers?▼
Princess Cruises better serves nervous first-timers through structured orientation, consistent service protocols, and intuitive ship layouts. The OceanMedallion eliminates friction points (room access, payments, locating companions), while traditional dining assignments provide social scaffolding. Princess's demographic skews older, reducing intimidation for mature first-timers. Norwegian suits confident beginners seeking immediate cruise immersion without etiquette learning curves. Freestyle Cruising removes anxiety about dining times and dress codes; the casual atmosphere feels instantly accessible. Norwegian's entertainment density prevents boredom during sea days. First-timers prioritising comfort and guidance choose Princess; those wanting independence and energy select Norwegian. Both offer excellent value introductory itineraries from European homeports.
Main differences?▼
The fundamental divergence centres on structure versus freedom. Princess preserves cruise traditions: optional formal nights, fixed or assigned dining, enrichment programming, and refined atmosphere. Norwegian demolished these conventions through Freestyle Cruising—no dress codes, no dining times, no assigned tables, no pressure. Architecturally, Princess favours elegant, understated interiors; Norwegian embraces bold, Las Vegas-inspired spectacle with outdoor promenades and adrenaline attractions. Demographically, Princess attracts 55+ established cruisers; Norwegian captures 35-55 first-timers and families. Technology differs: Princess's OceanMedallion enables seamless personalization; Norwegian focuses on entertainment tech. Service style contrasts—Princess offers anticipatory attentiveness; Norwegian provides efficient informality. Neither superior absolutely; alignment with personal travel philosophy determines satisfaction.
