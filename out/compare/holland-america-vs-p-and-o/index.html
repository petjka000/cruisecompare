Holland America Line vs P&O Cruises 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Holland America Line vs P&O Cruises 2026

Holland America Line and P&O Cruises represent two distinct approaches to mainstream cruising. Holland America, with its 150-year Dutch heritage, emphasises refined elegance, destination immersion and an older, internationally-minded clientele. P&O Cruises, Britain's favourite cruise line, delivers quintessential British cruising with familiar comforts, entertainment and exceptional value. Both operate mid-sized ships but serve different markets: Holland America attracts well-travelled North Americans and international guests seeking cultural enrichment, whilst P&O dominates the UK market with homeport convenience and British hospitality. This comparison examines pricing, onboard experience, dining, entertainment and suitability for different traveller profiles to determine which line best matches your 2026 holiday expectations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
P&O Cruises generally offers superior value for British travellers, particularly when factoring in included gratuities (saving €112 per week), frequent promotions with free parking or onboard credit, and lower drinks package prices at €45 versus Holland America's €55 daily. However, Holland America's fares include more enrichment activities and culinary experiences that P&O charges extra for. For fly-cruises, Holland America's international pricing can be competitive, but P&O's no-fly Southampton departures eliminate flight costs entirely. Ultimately, value depends on your spending patterns: P&O suits those wanting predictable costs, whilst Holland America rewards guests who utilise included programming extensively.
Which for families?▼
P&O Cruises decisively wins for families with children of any age. Their night nursery operates free until 2am for ages 6 months-4 years, whilst kids' clubs cater to 2-17 year olds with age-appropriate activities and secure registration. Family cabins, interconnecting rooms, and child-friendly dining options including early tea service make logistics effortless. Holland America accepts children but offers minimal programming, no nursery, and an adult-oriented atmosphere where youngsters may feel out of place. P&O's larger ships also feature water parks, cinemas and sports courts that engage teenagers, whilst Holland America's smaller vessels lack comparable facilities.
How do prices compare?▼
P&O Cruises typically undercuts Holland America by 10-15% on comparable seven-night itineraries, with inside cabins averaging €699 versus €799. This gap widens when accounting for gratuities: P&O includes these in fares, whilst Holland America adds €16 daily per person. Drinks packages show similar disparity at €45 versus €55 daily. However, Holland America frequently offers promotional upgrades and onboard credits that narrow this difference. Shore excursions cost comparably, though Holland America's longer port stays may reduce excursion necessity. Fly-cruise pricing varies seasonally, but P&O's no-fly advantage typically saves £200-400 per couple in airfares.
Which has better food?▼
Holland America Line delivers superior culinary quality through its Culinary Arts Centre partnership, fresh regional sourcing, and dedicated fleet of culinary-trained chefs. The line's commitment to destination-inspired menus, cooking demonstrations and wine pairings creates genuine gastronomic interest. P&O Cruises provides solid, crowd-pleasing British fare—roast dinners, curries, afternoon tea—executed reliably but without ambition. Specialty restaurants on both lines incur surcharges, though Holland America's Pinnacle Grill and Tamarind offer more distinctive experiences than P&O's equivalent venues. Vegetarian and dietary options are well-handled by both, but Holland America's flexibility and creativity edge ahead for discerning palates.
Which for first-timers?▼
P&O Cruises proves more accessible for British first-timers through Southampton departures eliminating flight anxiety, familiar cultural references throughout, and straightforward pricing with fewer hidden extras. The larger ships feel less intimidating with more public spaces to explore, whilst the entertainment programme requires no prior cruising knowledge to enjoy. Holland America's international passenger mix and more formal atmosphere can feel daunting initially, though their exceptional service standards and smaller ships ultimately create loyal repeaters. First-timers prioritising convenience and comfort should choose P&O; those seeking distinctive, refined experiences may appreciate Holland America's unique character despite the steeper learning curve.
Main differences?▼
The fundamental distinction lies in cultural orientation: Holland America pursues international sophistication with Dutch-American heritage, whilst P&O embodies British mainstream cruising. Operationally, Holland America favours smaller ships (1,432-2,650 guests) with longer port stays and enrichment programming; P&O operates larger vessels (1,800-5,200 guests) prioritising sea-day entertainment. Demographically, Holland America skews older and more affluent; P&O spans broader ages with stronger family appeal. Financially, P&O offers transparent value with more inclusions; Holland America presents premium positioning with à la carte pricing. Geographically, Holland America dominates Alaska and exotic itineraries; P&O specialises in Mediterranean, Caribbean and Norwegian fjords from Southampton.
