MSC Cruises vs Princess Cruises 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > MSC Cruises vs Princess Cruises 2026

MSC Cruises and Princess Cruises represent two distinct approaches to contemporary cruising, both positioned in the mid-market segment but appealing to different demographics. MSC, Europe's largest cruise line, brings Mediterranean flair, multi-generational programming, and competitive pricing to its expanding fleet. Princess, Carnival Corporation's premium brand, emphasizes destination immersion, culinary excellence, and mature sophistication. For 2026 sailings, both lines operate modern fleets with strong Caribbean and Mediterranean deployment, yet their onboard cultures diverge significantly—MSC prioritizes lively entertainment and family value, while Princess focuses on enrichment, relaxation, and destination-focused itineraries. Understanding these distinctions ensures travellers select the experience aligning with their vacation priorities and travel style.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
MSC Cruises typically delivers superior overall value, particularly for European and Mediterranean sailings where base fares often include more promotional incentives. Their aggressive pricing strategy, frequent status match programmes, and lower onboard costs for beverages and specialty dining create compelling propositions. Princess Cruises, while occasionally cheaper on headline Caribbean fares, tends to generate higher total expenditure through premium-priced add-ons and stronger encouragement to upgrade experiences. For travellers content with included amenities, MSC's value proposition strengthens; those prioritising culinary and service quality may find Princess worth the premium. Consider total vacation cost including gratuities, Wi-Fi, and beverages when comparing identical itineraries.
Which for families?▼
MSC Cruises decisively wins for families, offering complimentary kids' clubs from birth through seventeen with dedicated facilities, qualified multilingual staff, and flexible drop-off hours. Their ships feature extensive water parks, bowling alleys, F1 simulators, and family cabin configurations unavailable on Princess. The Doremi programme operates daily until midnight, accommodating European dining schedules. Princess Cruises provides Camp Discovery with strong partnerships (Discovery, Animal Planet) but restricts nursery services, limits teen programming, and offers fewer adrenaline activities. MSC's family pricing—children under eighteen sail free on select fares—further cements advantage for multi-generational travel.
How do prices compare?▼
Princess Cruises generally advertises lower base fares for Caribbean and Alaska itineraries, with seven-night inside cabins averaging €699 versus MSC's €799. However, MSC frequently offers promotions including beverage packages, Wi-Fi, and shore excursion credits that Princess sells separately. MSC's Easy Plus beverage package (€38/day) undercuts Princess Premier (€55/day), while gratuities run €21 lower weekly. Princess specialty dining commands premium pricing (€25-65 versus €15-45). European deployments reverse this pattern—MSC's home-market advantage delivers consistently lower pricing. Always compare identical dates, cabin categories, and included amenities for accurate assessment.
Which has better food?▼
Princess Cruises maintains superior culinary reputation across mainstream cruising, with consistently prepared dishes, quality ingredients, and thoughtful menu development. Their partnership with chocolatier Norman Love, Alfredo's Pizzeria (complimentary), and International Café elevate casual dining. MSC Cruises has improved significantly, particularly in Yacht Club and specialty venues, but main dining room consistency varies by ship and itinerary. MSC's strength lies in authentic international cuisine reflecting their Mediterranean heritage—superior pasta, gelato, and European preparations—while Princess excels at American-comfort classics and seafood execution. Food enthusiasts prioritising reliability should select Princess; adventurous diners appreciating European flavours may prefer MSC.
Which for first-timers?▼
Princess Cruises suits first-timers seeking predictable, polished introduction to cruising with intuitive service model, consistent quality, and supportive environment for learning cruise protocols. Their MedallionClass technology simplifies embarkation, cabin access, and onboard navigation. MSC Cruises appeals to adventurous first-timers comfortable with multilingual environments, flexible dining, and energetic atmospheres—particularly younger travellers or Europeans familiar with the brand. Princess' structured programming builds cruising confidence; MSC's value proposition reduces financial risk if the experience disappoints. Consider departure port convenience and itinerary appeal equally, as these often outweigh line differences for novice cruisers.
Main differences?▼
MSC Cruises operates with European sensibility—multilingual announcements, later dining schedules, cosmopolitan passenger mix, and high-energy entertainment including acrobatic productions and nightclub culture. Their ships emphasise physical amenities: water parks, sports complexes, and expansive spa facilities. Princess Cruises embodies American premium cruising—earlier dining, enrichment-focused programming, destination immersion through expert lectures, and refined adult ambience. Their MedallionClass technology enables personalised service delivery unmatched in mainstream cruising. MSC prioritises family value and scale; Princess emphasises culinary quality and sophisticated relaxation. Both represent mid-market positioning, yet cultural orientation, service philosophy, and onboard energy create distinctly different vacation experiences.
