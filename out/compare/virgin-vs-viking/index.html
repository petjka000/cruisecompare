Virgin Voyages vs Viking Cruises 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Virgin Voyages vs Viking Cruises 2026

Virgin Voyages and Viking Cruises represent two distinct philosophies in premium cruising. Virgin Voyages targets adults 18+ with a contemporary, party-forward atmosphere, music festivals at sea, and Instagram-worthy design. Viking Cruises focuses on destination immersion, cultural enrichment, and quiet elegance with no casinos or children. Both position themselves above mass-market lines but below ultra-luxury, appealing to discerning travellers who prioritise either vibrant social energy or intellectual curiosity. Virgin's Scarlet Lady and Resilient Lady sail from Barcelona and Miami, while Viking's ocean fleet of identical 930-guest ships offers global itineraries with extended port stays. Your choice depends fundamentally on whether you seek stimulation or serenity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
Viking Cruises generally offers superior value through its comprehensive inclusions: beer and wine at lunch and dinner, one shore excursion in every port, and genuinely unlimited WiFi. Virgin Voyages appears competitively priced but nickel-and-dimes on extras—premium WiFi, shore excursions, spa treatments, and alcoholic beverages beyond basic sailaway packages add up rapidly. Virgin's base fare covers dining and tips, yet the total holiday cost often exceeds Viking when comparing equivalent experiences. Viking's transparent pricing model means fewer surprises at checkout. However, Virgin's inclusive dining with no supplemental charges for specialty restaurants provides value for food-focused travellers who would pay premiums elsewhere.
Which for families?▼
Neither cruise line accepts guests under 18, making this comparison moot for traditional families. For multi-generational adult groups—adult children with parents, sibling reunions, or friend groups travelling together—Virgin Voyages creates more dynamic shared experiences through its social programming and varied entertainment. Viking suits families seeking peaceful reunion time with minimal stimulation. Both lines specifically design for adult travellers without children's facilities, programmes, or dining accommodations. Families with minors should consider Disney Cruise Line, Royal Caribbean, or Norwegian Cruise Line instead. Adult family groups should assess whether they prefer Virgin's energetic togetherness or Viking's contemplative shared discovery.
How do prices compare?▼
Viking Cruises' brochure pricing typically starts 10-15% below Virgin Voyages for comparable itineraries, with Mediterranean seven-night voyages averaging €699 inside versus Virgin's €799. However, Virgin frequently runs aggressive promotional campaigns with substantial discounts, occasionally achieving price parity or undercutting Viking. Viking maintains more stable pricing with early booking incentives rather than deep discounting. When comparing total cost of holiday, Viking's included beverages and excursions often render it cheaper despite higher apparent base fares. Virgin's à-la-carte model suits travellers who minimise onboard spending; Viking rewards those who utilise inclusive offerings fully. Both position firmly above mass-market lines like Costa or MSC.
Which has better food?▼
Virgin Voyages dominates on culinary innovation and variety with 20+ distinct venues including Korean BBQ, a food hall with multiple concepts, experimental test kitchen, and Michelin-starred chef collaborations. The quality is genuinely impressive for a mid-market line, with no buffet and no main dining room creating restaurant-quality experiences throughout. Viking Cruises offers consistently excellent, refined cuisine with Scandinavian influences and destination-specific menus, but operates more traditionally with main restaurant, buffet, and limited specialty options. Viking's food satisfies sophisticated palates without surprising them; Virgin's risks inconsistency but achieves memorable highs. Food enthusiasts seeking discovery prefer Virgin; those valuing reliability and classical preparation prefer Viking.
Which for first-timers?▼
Viking Cruises provides the gentler introduction to cruising with intuitive ship layout, included excursions that eliminate decision paralysis, and staff trained to guide novices through protocols. The smaller ship size reduces overwhelm, and the cultured atmosphere attracts curious travellers without cruise experience. Virgin Voyages appeals to first-timers specifically resistant to traditional cruising—those who would never consider Carnival or Royal Caribbean. Its contemporary design, casual dress code, and elimination of cruise conventions (no announcements, no formal nights, no buffet queues) attracts cruise-sceptics. First-timers seeking classic cruise romance should choose Viking; those wanting a floating boutique hotel experience should choose Virgin.
Main differences?▼
The fundamental distinction lies in energy and intention: Virgin Voyages creates a self-contained entertainment destination where the ship matters as much as ports, while Viking Cruises treats the vessel as elegant transportation to cultural experiences. Virgin's Scarlet Lady carries 2,770 guests with nightclub atmosphere, tattoo parlours, and fitness focus; Viking's identical ocean ships carry 930 guests with Nordic spa, enrichment lectures, and destination dining. Virgin eliminates cruise traditions (no buffet, no main dining room, no dress codes); Viking preserves refined conventions without formality. Virgin targets millennials and Gen X seeking social connection; Viking attracts Boomers and Gen X seeking intellectual stimulation. Both exclude children and position above mass-market, yet serve entirely different traveller psychologies.
