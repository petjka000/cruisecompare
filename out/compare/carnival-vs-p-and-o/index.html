Carnival Cruise Line vs P&O Cruises 2026

An honest, detailed comparison to help you choose the right cruise line.

Compare > Carnival Cruise Line vs P&O Cruises 2026

Carnival Cruise Line and P&O Cruises represent two distinct approaches to mainstream cruising. Carnival, the world's largest cruise line, delivers high-energy, American-style fun with waterslides, elaborate shows, and party atmospheres. P&O Cruises, Britain's favourite cruise line, offers a more refined, traditional experience with afternoon tea, black-tie evenings, and understated entertainment. Both operate mid-market pricing but serve fundamentally different passenger expectations. Carnival dominates the Caribbean and short Bahamas itineraries from US ports, while P&O specialises in Mediterranean, Northern Europe, and world voyages from Southampton. Your choice depends entirely on whether you prefer lively, resort-style holidays or genteel, culturally British cruising.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better value?▼
P&O Cruises typically offers superior headline value for British travellers, with fares including gratuities, basic drinks, and flights where applicable. Their transparent pricing avoids the nickel-and-diming common on Carnival. However, Carnival's aggressive discounting, especially on Caribbean routes, can produce lower net costs for flexible bookers. Factor in Carnival's mandatory €16 daily service charges versus P&O's included tips, plus drinks package differences. Carnival's CHEERS! package at €65 daily exceeds P&O's more restrictive options. For families, Carnival's included kids' programming adds substantial value. Ultimately, P&O wins on pricing clarity and inclusions, while Carnival rewards savvy bargain-hunters willing to navigate add-on costs.
Which for families?▼
Carnival Cruise Line decisively wins for families, particularly those with children under 12. Their Camp Ocean programme operates from 2-17 with age-appropriate divisions, while Dr. Seuss at Sea provides character breakfasts, parades, and dedicated spaces. WaterWorks waterparks, mini-golf, and IMAX theatres keep children occupied throughout voyages. Family harbour cabins sleep five with connecting options. P&O's The Reef offers competent but limited children's programming, with smaller facilities and fewer dedicated family spaces. P&O's adult-oriented atmosphere and formal nights can constrain family flexibility. Choose Carnival for pure family holiday energy; select P&O only if your children appreciate quieter, more structured environments or you prioritise adult escape.
How do prices compare?▼
P&O Cruises' average seven-night inside cabin starts at €699, approximately €100 below Carnival's €799 equivalent. However, direct comparison proves misleading due to structural differences. P&O includes gratuities (saving €112 weekly), while Carnival adds €16 daily automatically. P&O's drinks packages start lower, though Carnival's CHEERS! offers broader inclusion. Carnival's Caribbean positioning enables competitive flash sales reaching €499 for seven nights, undercutting P&O's Mediterranean routes. British travellers face additional P&O advantages: no-flight Southampton departures eliminate air costs, while fare inclusions reduce onboard spending. American passengers encounter Carnival's home-port convenience versus P&O's positioning as a fly-cruise premium option. Calculate total trip costs including flights, transfers, and onboard spending for accurate comparison.
Which has better food?▼
P&O Cruises offers superior main dining room quality with consistent British standards, proper silver service, and menus reflecting culinary tradition. Their partnership with Marco Pierre White elevates specialty venues, while afternoon tea remains genuinely excellent. Carnival counters with greater variety: Guy Fieri's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, and Shaquille O'Neal's Big Chicken provide casual options absent from P&O. Carnival's buffet operates longer hours with more stations. However, Carnival's mass-production shows in main dining room consistency, with variable execution despite expanded choices. Foodies prefer P&O's disciplined approach; casual diners favour Carnival's abundance and flexibility. Neither competes with premium lines, but P&O's traditional execution edges Carnival's quantity-over-quality philosophy for discerning palates.
Which for first-timers?▼
Carnival Cruise Line serves first-timers better through its welcoming, low-pressure environment. The anything-goes atmosphere eliminates anxiety about dress codes, dining etiquette, or cruise conventions. Extensive included activities ensure first-timers discover preferences without additional cost. The party reputation, while sometimes overstated, signals permission to relax and experiment. P&O's traditional structure—formal nights, assigned dining, and British social codes—can intimidate uncertain newcomers. However, P&O suits first-timers seeking gentle introduction through Southampton convenience, included gratuities, and familiar cultural context. British travellers nervous about flying or foreign environments find P&O's domestic departure reassuring. Choose Carnival for carefree experimentation; select P&O if you value structure and familiar social frameworks for initial cruising confidence.
Main differences?▼
Atmosphere fundamentally distinguishes these lines: Carnival delivers high-energy American resort cruising with constant activity, loud music, and party culture; P&O provides restrained British tradition with formal nights, afternoon tea, and genteel entertainment. Operational models differ significantly—Carnival operates massive ships (6,500 passengers on Mardi Gras) with economies of scale; P&O maintains smaller, more intimate vessels (Arvia excepted). Carnival's Caribbean focus contrasts P&O's European specialisation. Pricing transparency favours P&O with included gratuities; Carnival's à-la-carte approach enables lower base fares with add-on accumulation. Demographics diverge sharply: Carnival targets young families and party-seekers; P&O cultivates mature loyalists and solo travellers. These aren't minor variations but entirely different cruise philosophies requiring genuine self-assessment to choose correctly.
