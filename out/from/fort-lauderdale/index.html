About Fort Lauderdale (Port Everglades)

Port Everglades, located in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, is one of the busiest cruise ports in the world, handling approximately 4 million passengers annually. The port serves as a major homeport for Celebrity Cruises, Holland America Line, Princess Cruises, Royal Caribbean International, Carnival Cruise Line, and Disney Cruise Line. Situated on 17 acres along the Intracoastal Waterway with direct access to the Atlantic Ocean, Port Everglades offers modern terminal facilities that can accommodate some of the world's largest cruise ships. The port is renowned for its efficient embarkation and disembarkation processes, making it a preferred departure point for Caribbean cruises ranging from 3 to 14+ days. Fort Lauderdale's famous beach culture, upscale dining, and vibrant waterfront entertainment district are all within easy reach of the cruise terminals, allowing passengers to extend their vacation before or after their cruise.

🚗 Getting There & Away

From the Airport

Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL) is the closest airport, located just 3 miles (approximately 5 km) from Port Everglades. Taxis and ride-shares (Uber/Lyft) cost approximately €14-18 for the 10-15 minute journey. Miami International Airport (MIA) is about 30 miles away and may be cheaper for international arrivals, though the journey takes 45-60 minutes and costs approximately €45-55 by taxi. Many cruise passengers fly into FLL the day before their cruise to avoid any timing stress.

From City Centre

Fort Lauderdale's downtown area is approximately 2 miles from the cruise port. Taxis are readily available and cost approximately €9-14 for the short ride. The Sun Trolley offers a free Downtown Link service connecting the beach area to downtown, though it doesn't directly serve the port. Water taxis operate along the Intracoastal Waterway and can be a scenic option for reaching nearby hotels and attractions.

Parking

Port Everglades offers several parking options. The main cruise parking garage (located adjacent to Terminals 4 and 10) charges approximately €22-28 per day for self-parking. Premium parking closer to terminals runs approximately €32-40 per day. Off-site parking facilities near the port offer rates from €15-20 per day with complimentary shuttle transfers. Park Sleep Fly packages are available for those flying in the night before. Advance parking reservations are recommended during peak season (December through April).

Shuttles

Most major cruise lines offer dedicated airport-to-port shuttle services for approximately €25-35 per person one-way. These can be booked through the cruise line or as part of a cruise package. Port Everglades operates a free on-port shuttle service connecting the different terminals and parking areas. Many hotels along Fort Lauderdale Beach offer free cruise port shuttle services for their guests — always confirm availability when booking accommodation.

🏛️ Cruise Terminals

Terminal 1 (Princess Cruise Terminal)

Modern terminal facility primarily serving Princess Cruises. Features comfortable waiting areas, efficient check-in counters, and direct access to the berths. The terminal has undergone significant renovations to accommodate the line's larger Grand-class and Royal-class vessels.

Princess Cruises

Terminal 2 (Holland America Line Terminal)

Dedicated Holland America Line terminal with a distinctive design. Known for smooth embarkation processes and personalized service consistent with Holland America's traditional approach to cruising.

Holland America Line

Terminal 4 (Celebrity/Disney/Royal Caribbean Terminal)

One of the most modern terminals at Port Everglades, Terminal 4 serves multiple cruise lines including Celebrity Cruises and Royal Caribbean. Features high ceilings, natural lighting, and state-of-the-art security screening. Disney Cruise Line operates from this terminal during select periods.

Terminal 10 (Carnival Cruise Line Hub)

Carnival's dedicated terminal at Port Everglades, designed for high-volume operations. Features efficient throughput design allowing thousands of passengers to board simultaneously. The terminal reflects Carnival's fun-forward approach to cruising.

Port Everglades' southern terminals serve as the primary facility for Royal Caribbean's Oasis-class ships, Holland America's Rotterdam-class vessels, and additional Princess and Carnival operations. 🚌 Local Transport

Metro

Fort Lauderdale does not have a traditional metro or subway system. The nearest alternative rail service is the Brightline train (high-speed intercity rail) at the Fort Lauderdale station, located approximately 2 miles inland. Brightline connects Fort Lauderdale to Miami, West Palm Beach, and Orlando. The Tri-Rail commuter rail system has stations throughout South Florida but does not directly serve Port Everglades.

Bus

Broward County Transit (BCT) Route 1 runs along Federal Highway and provides service to Fort Lauderdale Beach. Route 11 serves the SE 17th Street corridor near the port. However, public bus service is limited and not typically recommended for cruise passengers with luggage. The Sun Trolley offers free and low-cost transportation along popular routes including downtown and the beach.

Taxi

Taxis are readily available at Port Everglades with posted rates. Standard fares within Fort Lauderdale typically range from €9-25 depending on distance. Taxis to Fort Lauderdale Beach cost approximately €14-18. rideshare services (Uber and Lyft) are also active in the area and often provide competitive pricing. Always confirm the fare before departing.

Walkability

Port Everglades cruise terminals are located on Eller Drive, which is primarily an industrial/commercial area. Fort Lauderdale Beach is approximately 1 mile (20-25 minute walk) from the port along SE 17th Street Causeway. Walking to Las Olas Boulevard takes about 30 minutes. The walk is pleasant along tree-lined streets but can be hot in summer months. Consider a taxi for comfort during Florida's warm season.

🎯 Nearby Attractions

Fort Lauderdale Beach & Las Olas Boulevard

Fort Lauderdale's iconic beach and main thoroughfare are approximately 1 mile from the port. Las Olas features upscale boutiques, art galleries, and restaurants. The beach itself offers white sand, calm Atlantic waters, and the famous Las Olas Beach Pavilion.

🚶 20-25 minutes or 5 minutes by taxi💰 Free to visit; dining varies €15-50 per person

Hugh Taylor Birch State Park

A 180-acre oceanfront park offering nature trails, canoeing, and kayaking. This hidden gem provides a peaceful escape from the cruise terminal bustle and showcases Florida's native ecosystems including coastal hammock and mangrove forest.

🚶 15 minutes from port💰 €7 vehicle entry, €2 per pedestrian/bicyclist

Stranahan House & Museum

Fort Lauderdale's oldest standing structure, built in 1901. This preserved home offers insight into the city's founding history and the relationship between early settlers and the native Seminole people. Located on the New River in downtown Fort Lauderdale.

🚶 15 minutes from port💰 €10 adults, €5 children

Broward Center for the Performing Arts

A world-class performing arts venue overlooking the New River. Even if you don't catch a show, the building's architecture and waterfront setting make it worth a visit. The adjacent Esplanade offers river views and outdoor seating.

🚶 15-20 minutes from port💰 Free to visit exterior; show prices vary €30-150

Bonnet House Museum & Gardens

A 35-acre historic estate featuring a unique blend of Art Deco and Caribbean architecture. The grounds include orchid greenhouses, art studios, and wildlife including Florida manatees and alligators in the surrounding waterways.

🚶 25 minutes by taxi💰 €15 adults, €12 students/seniors

Las Olas Oceanside Park

A waterfront park at the beach end of Las Olas Boulevard featuring outdoor fitness equipment, volleyball courts, a playground, and beach access. Great for a quick pre-cruise stroll or workout before boarding.

🚶 25-30 minutes from port💰 Free

🌤️ Best Time to Visit

Best Months: November through April offers the most comfortable cruising weather from Fort Lauderdale. These months feature temperatures of 20-28°C (68-82°F), low humidity, minimal rainfall, and calm seas. January and February are peak season with the most consistent weather but also the highest prices.

Peak Season: December through March is peak cruise season from Port Everglades. Holiday cruises (Christmas and New Year's) command premium pricing and fill months in advance. March Break (mid-February to mid-April) sees significant family traffic. Weather is generally excellent but prices are highest during these months.

Climate: Fort Lauderdale enjoys a tropical climate year-round. Summers (May-October) are hot and humid with afternoon thunderstorms common. Winter months are mild and dry. For Caribbean cruises from Fort Lauderdale, pack light summer clothes, swimwear, formal attire for elegant evenings, and sun protection including hats and SPF 50+ sunscreen. A light jacket is useful for air-conditioned ship interiors.

💡 Embarkation Tips

  • →Arrive at Port Everglades between 10:00-11:30 AM for the smoothest boarding experience — arriving too early often means standing in queue outside in the Florida heat, while arriving after 1:00 PM can mean longer waits at check-in.
  • →Terminal 4 has the most modern facilities and air conditioning — if your cruise line offers a choice of terminals, this is the preferable option for comfort during the boarding process.
  • →Download your cruise line's app before arriving and complete mobile check-in — this can significantly speed up the document verification step at Port Everglades where cellular service can be congested.
  • →Port Everglades' security is strict about what you can bring — coolers, large strollers, and oversized luggage may be stored in the baggage holding area if flagged, causing delays. Pack strategically or leave non-essentials in your car.
  • →Traffic on I-595 and Federal Highway approaching Port Everglades can gridlock during peak boarding times (11:00 AM-1:00 PM). Consider using the Port's official address (1850 Eller Drive, Fort Lauderdale, FL 33316) for GPS and approach from the south via I-95 Exit 23 if coming from Miami.

🧳 Insider Tips

  • ★Book a pre-cruise lunch at Coconuts or Boathouse at the Fort Lauderdale Beach while waiting for your embarkation time — these waterfront restaurants on the Intracoastal offer excellent seafood with views of mega-yachts and the cruise ships departing. You can watch the ships maneuver through the channel while you eat.
  • ★Save approximately 40% on parking by booking through Port Everglades' official parking partner in advance online rather than driving up. Look for the ParkWhiz or similar platforms that interface with the port's reservation system.
  • ★Skip the overpriced airport shuttles if arriving at FLL — the taxi stand at the airport terminal consistently has vehicles waiting, and the flat rate to Port Everglades (approximately €14) is often cheaper than shuttle group rates and far more convenient.

Frequently Asked Questions

How early should I arrive at Port Everglades for my cruise?▼
Most cruise lines recommend arriving at Port Everglades between 10:00 AM and 2:00 PM on embarkation day. Check-in typically opens 3 hours before departure. Arriving before 10:00 AM usually means waiting outside in the Florida heat, while arriving after 2:00 PM risks missing the all-aboard time. If driving yourself, factor in parking time which can add 30-45 minutes during busy periods.
Can I walk from Fort Lauderdale Beach to Port Everglades?▼
Yes, the walk from Fort Lauderdale Beach (Las Olas Boulevard area) to Port Everglades is approximately 1 mile and takes 20-25 minutes along SE 17th Street. The route is generally pedestrian-friendly but exposed to sun and heat. It's a viable option if you're staying at a beach hotel and want to skip a taxi, but not recommended with heavy luggage or during hot summer afternoons (noon-4 PM).
What is the cost of parking at Port Everglades?▼
Port Everglades' main parking garage charges approximately €22-28 per day for self-parking during peak season. Off-site parking lots in the vicinity offer lower rates from €15-20 per day with complimentary shuttle service. Both rates are significantly lower than the terminal-adjacent parking. Online pre-booking through the port's website or partner apps offers the best rates and guarantees a space during busy periods.
Which terminal will my cruise ship depart from at Port Everglades?▼
Terminal assignments are determined by cruise line and ship size, not by advance booking. You'll receive your terminal assignment in your final cruise documents (typically 3-5 days before departure) or through the cruise line's app. Major lines like Carnival operate primarily from Terminal 10, Royal Caribbean from Terminals 4 and 19, Princess from Terminals 1 and 29, and Holland America from Terminals 2 and 29. Always verify your terminal before departing for the port.
