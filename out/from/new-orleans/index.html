About New Orleans

New Orleans stands as one of the Gulf Coast's premier cruise departure ports, welcoming over 1 million passengers annually through its modern Julia Street Cruise Terminal complex. The port serves as homeport for major cruise lines including Carnival Cruise Line, Royal Caribbean, Norwegian Cruise Line, and Disney Cruise Line, offering itineraries to the Western Caribbean, Mexico, and the Bahamas. What makes New Orleans uniquely special is that passengers can combine their cruise vacation with authentic Southern hospitality, world-renowned jazz, and exceptional Creole cuisine—all within walking distance of the cruise terminal. The city transforms into a massive pre-cruise destination where passengers can explore the historic French Quarter, sample beignets at Café du Monde, or ride the historic St. Charles Streetcar before embarkation.

🚗 Getting There & Away

From the Airport

Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport (MSY) is located 25 km west of the cruise terminal. The most convenient options are: Taxis (flat rate €32-37) available from the airport's ground transportation level; Ride-share services (Uber/Lyft) cost €25-32; Airport shuttle services like Airport Shuttle New Orleans offer shared rides for approximately €18-22 per person; Private car services can be booked in advance for €50-70. Travel time from MSY to the cruise terminal is typically 30-45 minutes depending on traffic, though allow extra time during rush hour (7-9 AM and 4-7 PM).

From City Centre

The cruise terminals are exceptionally walkable from downtown New Orleans. The Julia Street Terminal is approximately 0.8 km (10-minute walk) from the French Quarter, accessible via the scenic Riverfront Walk along the Mississippi. From Jackson Square, head south on Decatur Street to the riverfront, then west along the promenade. Taxis from the French Quarter to the port cost approximately €9-14. The St. Charles Streetcar does not directly serve the port but stops at the edge of the Warehouse District, a 5-minute walk away.

Parking

On-site parking at the Port of New Orleans is available at the Julia Street Terminal parking garage. Daily rates are approximately €23-28 for covered parking, with discounted pre-booking available through the port website. Off-site parking lots near the cruise terminal offer lower rates around €14-18 per day with free shuttle to the terminal. Park 'N Fly facilities on Callender Street provide another budget option at €10-13 daily.

Shuttles

Most major cruise lines do not operate dedicated airport-to-port shuttles from MSY. However, shared-ride airport shuttles (Airport Shuttle New Orleans) can be booked to drop off at cruise terminals. Many hotels in the French Quarter and Warehouse District offer free cruise terminal transfers—book these when making hotel reservations. The port provides no official shuttle service, but private transfers can be arranged through cruise line partner programs.

🏛️ Cruise Terminals

Julia Street Cruise Terminal (Terminal 1)

The main cruise terminal at Port of New Orleans, located at 1350 Port of New Orleans Place. Features a modern two-story facility with spacious check-in halls, baggage handling areas, and direct pedestrian access to the riverfront. This is the primary homeport for Carnival and Royal Caribbean vessels.

🚌 Local Transport

Metro

New Orleans does not have a traditional metro/subway system. The New Orleans Regional Transit Authority (RTA) operates bus routes and the historic St. Charles Streetcar line. The St. Charles Streetcar is the most tourist-friendly option, running from the French Quarter through the Garden District to Carrollton.

Bus

RTA buses serve the port area with routes 55 (Farrow), 57 (Franklin), and 88 (L.C.L.A.) stopping near the cruise terminals. The RTA offers a $3 day pass valid on all buses and streetcars. However, the compact downtown area is largely walkable, and most attractions are within 20-minute walking distance of the port.

Taxi

Taxis are readily available throughout the downtown area. Flag fall is approximately €2.50 with €2.30 per kilometer thereafter. From the cruise terminal to the French Quarter costs €9-14; to the Garden District €14-18; to Airport €32-37. Ride-share apps (Uber, Lyft) operate extensively and often provide cheaper fares during off-peak hours.

Walkability

The Port of New Orleans is one of the most walkable cruise ports in the Americas. The Julia Street Terminal is less than 1 km from the French Quarter, connected by a pleasant riverside promenade along the Mississippi. Most major attractions (Bourbon Street, Jackson Square, Café du Monde, Jackson Brewery) are reachable in 10-20 minutes on foot. The Warehouse District galleries and Magazine Street shops are also within comfortable walking distance.

🎯 Nearby Attractions

French Quarter

The historic heart of New Orleans, famous for Bourbon Street, Jackson Square, and St. Louis Cathedral. Pre-cruise exploration is essential—sample beignets, browse antique shops, and absorb the live jazz spilling from every doorway.

🚶 12 minutes from Julia Street Terminal💰 Free to explore; food/drinks €10-40

Café du Monde

The iconic 24-hour café serving famous café au lait and beignets covered in powdered sugar since 1862. A non-negotiable New Orleans ritual—open from 8 AM to 5 AM daily.

🚶 15 minutes from port💰 €8-15 per person for coffee and beignets

National WWII Museum

America's premier WWII museum, ranked among the top museums globally. Features immersive exhibits, flight simulators, and extensive artifact collections. Plan 3-4 hours minimum.

🚶 8 minutes from Erato Street Terminal💰 Adults €32-38; students/seniors €25-30

Jackson Square

Historic public square bordered by the St. Louis Cathedral, Presbytere, and Spanish Colonial architecture. Street performers, artists, and fortune tellers create constant entertainment.

🚶 12 minutes from port💰 Free; donations appreciated

Garden District

Stunning residential neighborhood known for antebellum mansions, oak-lined streets, and the famous Lafayette Cemetery No. 1. Take the St. Charles streetcar line (€1.50) for a scenic 20-minute ride.

🚶 Streetcar from Magazine Street: 15 minutes💰 Streetcar fare €1.50; walking tour free

Steamboat NATCHEZ

Historic steamboat offering jazz brunches and dinner cruises on the Mississippi River, departing from the Toulouse Street Wharf just steps from the French Quarter.

🚶 10 minutes from port💰 Brunch cruise €55-70; dinner cruise €75-95

🌤️ Best Time to Visit

Best Months: The optimal cruising window from New Orleans is October through early December and March through May. These months offer temperatures of 18-26°C, lower humidity, and minimal rainfall. November deserves special mention—comfortable temperatures, manageable crowds, and the city buzzing with cultural festivals.

Peak Season: Peak cruise season runs November through March, coinciding with northern winter. Holiday sailings (Christmas, New Year's) command premium prices and fill months in advance. February and March are consistently busy months with many itineraries. Summer sailings (June-August) are less common due to temperatures regularly exceeding 32°C with 80%+ humidity.

Climate: New Orleans features a humid subtropical climate with mild winters (8-16°C) and hot, humid summers (25-35°C). For pre and post-cruise exploration, pack layers for variable conditions—air conditioning inside contrasts sharply with outdoor heat. Rain is possible year-round, so a compact umbrella is essential. Comfortable walking shoes are mandatory. Summer cruisers should bring sun protection and stay hydrated.

💡 Embarkation Tips

  • →Arrive in New Orleans the night before your cruise—downtown hotels within walking distance of the port (like the Hyatt Regency, Marriott, or HI New Orleans) average €120-180/night, and this eliminates early morning stress and guarantees you'll make embarkation even with flight delays.
  • →The Port of New Orleans opens for embarkation around 10:30-11:00 AM, but boarding groups are called randomly—so arriving at 11:30 AM can actually mean shorter waits than arriving at opening time when everyone crowds in. Most passengers finish boarding by 2:30 PM.
  • →Bypass the crowded terminal coffee shops and walk 2 blocks to MoPho (425 Magazine Street) for exceptional Vietnamese-Creole fusion or 4 blocks to Cochon (930 Tchoupitoulas Street) for James Beard Award-winning Southern cuisine—both are within easy walking distance and make excellent pre-cruise lunch spots.
  • →If driving, the Poydras Street entrance to the port parking garage is often less congested than the Julia Street entrance during peak arrival times. Coming from the west on I-10, exit at Poydras Street/St. Charles Avenue and approach from the river side.
  • →Luggage valet services are available for approximately €12-18 per bag—well worth it if you're arriving downtown early and don't want to drag bags around the French Quarter. Look for the designated cruise luggage check area near the terminal entrance.

🧳 Insider Tips

  • ★The New Orleans Cruise Terminal is directly across the street from The Outlet Collection at Riverwalk—a massive outlet mall that opens at 10 AM. If you have an early arrival and a late boarding time, store your carry-on bags at the port's luggage services (€8-12) and browse the mall or grab lunch at The Huck Finn Buffet inside while you wait.
  • ★Book a jazz brunch on the Steamboat NATCHEZ for your arrival morning if you're flying in the night before—it's a quintessential New Orleans experience and the 2-hour cruise provides a completely different perspective of the city and the Mississippi River.
  • ★Skip the overpriced tourist traps on Bourbon Street for food and head to the Warehouse District or Magazine Street. Dooky Chase's Restaurant (2371 St. Claude Avenue), Willie Mae's Scotch House (2381 St. Ann Street), and Mother’s Restaurant (401 Poydras Street) offer authentic Creole and Southern cuisine at a fraction of tourist area prices. Reservations strongly recommended for Mother's, which has been operating since 1937.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the address for the New Orleans cruise terminal?▼
The main Julia Street Cruise Terminal is located at 1350 Port of New Orleans Place, New Orleans, LA 70130. The Erato Street Terminal is at 1 Erato Street, New Orleans, LA 70130. Both are in the Warehouse District, approximately 1 km from the French Quarter.
How early should I arrive at the port on embarkation day?▼
The port typically begins boarding around 10:30-11:00 AM, with most passengers onboard by 2:30 PM. There's no advantage to arriving at opening—boarding groups are called randomly, so arriving between 11:00 AM and 1:00 PM often results in shorter initial wait times. Ensure you have your cruise documents ready and arrive no later than 2:00 PM to allow time for security and customs processing.
Is parking available at the New Orleans cruise terminal?▼
Yes, the Port of New Orleans operates a parking garage adjacent to the Julia Street Terminal with approximately 1,400 spaces. Covered parking costs approximately €23-28 per day when booked in advance through the port website. Off-site parking lots within walking distance offer lower rates around €10-18 per day with free shuttle service. Park 'N Fly (800 Josephine Street) and other nearby facilities are popular alternatives.
Can I walk from the cruise terminal to the French Quarter?▼
Absolutely—the New Orleans cruise port is one of the most walkable in North America. The Julia Street Terminal is less than 1 km (approximately 10-15 minutes) from the heart of the French Quarter. Follow the Riverfront Walk along the Mississippi south from Julia Street, then head north into the historic district. Jackson Square and Bourbon Street are easily reachable on foot.
